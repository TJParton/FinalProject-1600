Editing Audio Basic Info

Editing audio is essential if you wish to have a distinct and authentic sounding recording. You can edit out pauses and mistakes that you make which allows you to continue recording and then go back and fix it later.

Editing Audio Tools

  • Selection Tool, with the selection tool you can left click and drag to select a portion of audio that you can then work on individually
  • Envelope Tool, with the envelope tool you can left click and drag to select a portion of audio and then drag upwards to make it louder and downwards to make it quieter. regarding the envelope tool, you can add envelope points which then allows you to fade in/out your audio
  • Zoom Tool, with the zoom tool you can left click to zoom in on the audio and right click to zoom out
  • Draw Tool, with the draw tool you can drag specific points of audio up or down to remove clicks or to make certain points louder
  • Multi-Tool, with the multi-tool you can do everything in one, it is essentially a coalescence of all of the tools. Although, this is recommended for the more advanced user
